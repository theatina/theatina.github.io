# EXPOSED

Sitting on the sofa of the library

**..in Northern Ireland..**

being in contact with someone I really like ..

having a piano just two floors away..

but still trying to keep up with a coursework **I know ** I cannot properly complete.

That is the most unpleasant and stressful situation I can so far think of.

I am really happy and satisfied with my life right now, really. Only a few things can ruin all that. Like this situation. Now. Here.

I don't say that I don't like what I do. I chosed it. I am the only one to blame. The thing is, I am thinking of all the activities I would rather do right now, instead of enjoying what I am doing. I regret leaving people and events behind. I have done that before. The difference lies to the reason ***why** *I am doing it. Regrets appear when you think the sacrifice made is not worth , in the end.

Still here, searching staff for the first time, getting into paths I don't know where they end, swimming in the rough waters, in a stormy  weather, in a not -at- all cosy ambiance.

**

*A little help right here?*

**

That question goes for me , only. That is the point. Struggling is not life. And we love life, don't we? That is what I want to believe, at least.

Besides, trying to do is always the case, right?

What's the point of the purpose of life being a simple, easy, common thing?!

Doing, is the meaning of existence.

No matter how it goes or how it ends up.

DO

That is , devote yourself, even if it is the simplest thing.

Know what, where, when and why you do it.

Have a reason.

Not for others

**just for you**

Eat with your imagination

Drink with your feelings

Speak with your mind

Hear with your heart.

The tree is decorated in this floor, the library. It looks fabulous- the lights are blue, the ornaments white.

People, headphones, laptops, desktops, lovers, friends, fatigue, excitement, loneliness, romance.

You can spot everything around.

I could spend hours just looking aroung. Analysing the data, as my IT friends say.

Observing life, while living my own in the way I want. Each moment. Each second.

You know, I feel bad sometimes for the work I have to do. For that report, the revision or the exercise I have to spend hours on. Actually I feel bad for not putting an end to this horrible feeling of regret, being stressed till the submission day, or after getting the mark and thinking I should have done better, as I had already sacrificed things I love more than that.

It is only my fault, I have already accepted that.

All those perfectly organised gtherings, meetings, or whatever is going on the last days.

Coming back to my current situation.

I miss people, back home, or even here.

I miss not being myself.

*just thinking that my Erasmus trip is a success so far, having realised what I want to do-maybe mostly not do, and who I want to become, at least the current period of my meaningful life.*

**

So I was saying that

I miss that first week. ok maybe those three first weeks here, meeting new people, being introduced to the new thing of foreign studies, sharing, photographing with no limits, having time to relax, and do your work as you would like to, in your own tempo and terms. Let's say

*in yout style!*

**

Isn't it crazy that during those festive and happy moments, my heart seeks for everything but what I have? I can hear you, selfishness and negative thinking.. they are too loud to be ignored.

Another thing.

This experience, and now I know better, is all about finding a way to communicate with your own self at first, as a friend of mine told me once.

Go for what you didn't know, and find out if you like, how, where, when and whom you like sharing it with. unless you don't feel like trying. this is your choice

...and I am here now, still in the library, having met so many of my friends here, having to turn down musical nights, decide what to do the next day, being prepared for a concert .. ** bla bla bla bla bla**
