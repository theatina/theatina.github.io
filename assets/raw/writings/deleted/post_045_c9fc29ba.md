# Yes to us! 

Plenty of us think in a way we shouldn't.

Perhaps.

We sometimes say,  "and what if they.. he  she...?"

"I can't stand it anymore "

" I wish "

And many many many other excuses,  just to avoid **acting **or **doing **a step forward.

Just not to make a change, that *may* hurt people.

But what about us?

Is it selfish,  caring about our desires and more importantly,  our needs??

OK,  if you are not able to fly,  live with it.

You may succeed in the future but now the most possible scenario is to get killed.

I'm not talking about things you cannot, really cannot do.

What about your own life?

Of course you have people you love,  care and would die for.

Passions,  hobbies,  projects that we're dedicated to.

The thing is,  probably,  as I now have noticed, we use wrong words.

Of course social needs exist as well,  but still,  when we like the company of someone,  and then get attached..  It's not that we need them.

We **want **them.

No matter how.

We long for their company,  hug,  voice,  touch...  Figure,  around us..

All that is called desire. Not need.

Water is a need. Food is a need.

Instinct. Animal-like nature.

We happen to have something more than the rest of the animals. Probably we are more selfish. No way more intelligent. Otherwise we would be civilised.

However,  that something more we have acquired,  that evolution,  gives us the opportunity to prove we worth it.

By making more progress.

By accepting our past.

Living our present.

Dreaming and working about our future.

People can call people selfish beings.

I guess we are. But that's how we can all be happy.

Not by harming people. On the contrary by having a constant connection with our inner self. By listening and understanding ourselves. What our body,  mind and soul search for. So, by not hurting ourselves in order to be someone's else's blank spaces cover.

*After all,  when you say "yes"  to something,  you instantly and unavoidably say "no"  to something else*.

We were born. We're going to die.

In between  we are supposed to live.

Live **our **life. Not the neighbour's

Not our parents'.

..

I don't remember how old I was when I first started thinking about what others would say about my choices.

I just regret doing so...

Since then,  being judged was an inseparable part of life.

Caring or not, on the other hand, a *switch going ***on ***and ***off.**..
